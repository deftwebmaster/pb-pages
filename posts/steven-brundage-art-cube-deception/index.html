🧊 The Cube as a Canvas for Modern Magic#

Steven Brundage doesn’t just solve Rubik’s Cubes—he makes them solve themselves. He turns mathematical precision into visual poetry, combining world-class speedcubing with cunning misdirection and surgical performance design. The result: effects so clean, so baffling, even seasoned magicians get fooled.

In this post, we peel back the layers of his three most iconic routines—Cube Solve, Insta-Solve, and Behind-the-Back Solve—and reveal the hidden choreography that transforms puzzle-solving into pure astonishment.

1. 🟨 Cube Solve (Matching Cubes)#

🧠 The Illusion#

A spectator scrambles a cube. Brundage produces his own cube, and it perfectly matches theirs—even though he never seems to look at it. Sometimes it happens behind someone’s back. Sometimes inside a bag.

The implication? Either telepathy or a miracle.

🔍 The Method#

  • Brundage examines the spectator’s mixed cube (often while joking or misdirecting).
  • He then rapidly executes a memorized sequence to match it on his own cube.
  • His cube was likely in a neutral or pre-set state, ready to adapt.

🎩 Variations#

In stage or TV settings, Brundage may use:

  • A third cube in a memorized “fake scramble”
  • A subtle switch inside a bag
  • A dramatic crumpling of the bag to hide a duplicate

🧠 Psychological Framing#

Brundage leans into the mathematical improbability. He mentions “billions of combinations” to push the audience into focusing on probability—not preparation. This is misdirection of suspicion: it diverts their thoughts from how to how could that even be possible?

2. ⚡ Insta-Solve (Instant Solve)#

🔮 The Effect#

A cube is scrambled, then—in a snap—it’s solved:

  • Mid-air
  • Inside a bag
  • With a color shift

Audiences are left thinking: Did I even blink?

⚙️ The Method#

  • False Scramble: Cube appears mixed but is actually only a few moves away from solved.
  • Brundage memorizes and reverses those moves at high speed.
  • During a toss or gesture, he executes the reverse steps—often in less than a second.

🧰 Variations#

  • Bag Solve: Silent cubes allow him to manipulate the cube inside the bag.
  • Toss Solve: The solve happens in midair.
  • Color Change Solve: Uses props like thermal “Phantom” cubes or lenticular tiles.

🧠 Temporal Misdirection#

The trick works because the real move happens when no one’s watching. The bag, the air toss, or a flick of the wrist becomes the mask for the method. Your brain fills in the impossible.

3. 🤯 Behind-the-Back One-Hand Solve#

🧨 The Effect#

He tosses a mixed cube behind his back, catches it solved—all with one hand.

🔍 The Mechanics#

  • The cube starts partially solved (false scramble).
  • Brundage executes a precise one-handed algorithm during the toss or behind-the-back moment.
  • Years of speedcubing drills give him the speed and muscle memory to pull it off flawlessly.

🧠 The Real Magic#

  • No switches. No gimmicks. Just pure skill.
  • The trick succeeds because the execution is faster than suspicion.
  • He shows solved sides first—leveraging selective revelation.

4. The Invisible Arsenal: Principles in Play#

🎯 False Scrambles#

A cube looks random but is secretly solvable in 6–9 moves. Brundage uses these to set up Insta-Solves and Toss Solves.

🧠 Algorithmic Muscle Memory#

All his matching and solving work comes from speedcubing expertise, not trick cubes. His hands know hundreds of move sets.

👀 Misdirection (of both eyes and mind)#

  • Attention misdirection: “Don’t blink!”
  • Suspicion misdirection: “There are billions of combinations…”

🕰️ Temporal Misdirection#

Crucial moves happen when no one’s paying attention (e.g., bag moment, toss arc).

🧍 Performance Persona#

Brundage comes off as laid-back, charming, humble—making you trust him. That lowers audience defenses, increasing deception.

5. Tools vs. Gimmicks: A Nuanced Truth#

Brundage often claims: “No gimmicks. The cube is fully examinable.”

Technically, that’s true:

  • The cube itself is usually ungimmicked.
  • But the routine is carefully built around pre-set conditions, prepared scrambles, and sometimes cube switches.

His real “gimmick” is the framing. He convinces the audience that it’s just him and the cube—no trickery—when in fact, every moment is engineered to appear casual while hiding precision.

6. Lessons for Magicians#

🔄 The Illusion of Impossibility#

Brundage’s effects feel impossible—but they’re built on:

  • Years of cube-handling experience
  • Carefully choreographed reveals
  • Deep audience psychology

🧠 Magic Isn’t Just a Secret—It’s a Strategy#

You don’t just learn a trick. You learn how to:

  • Control perception
  • Direct thought
  • Exploit assumptions

🔁 Gimmicks Are Optional—Skill Isn’t#

Whether you use an ungimmicked cube or a lenticular color-changer, the real magic is in how you sell it.

🔚 Conclusion: When Effort Disguises Itself as Wonder#

Steven Brundage’s magic is not about electronics or gimmicked props. It’s about practice disguised as impossibility, and skill concealed within personality. He brings together:

  • The brute memorization of hundreds of cube algorithms
  • The stagecraft of misdirection and audience framing
  • The precision of a technician with the flair of a showman

Brundage proves that even with the most common object—a Rubik’s Cube—you can create modern miracles, as long as you control what people see… and what they think they saw.
